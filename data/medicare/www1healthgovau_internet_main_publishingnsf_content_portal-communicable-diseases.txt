### [Australian Gonococcal Surveillance Programme (AGSP) annual
reports](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-pubs-annlrpt-gonoanrep.htm)

24 February 2023

The Australian Gonococcal Surveillance Programme is funded by the Federal
health department to provide laboratory analysis of gonococcal isolates in
Australia. The Programme produces an annual report on its findings as well as
a report on the WHO Western Pacific Region Gonococcal Antimicrobial
Surveillance Programme. This page links to annual reports published since
1996. These annual reports are available in PDF format and, for accessibility,
in either HTML or Microsoft Word format.

### [Australian Meningococcal Surveillance Programme (AMSP) annual
reports](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-pubs-annlrpt-menganrep.htm)

24 February 2023

The Australian Meningococcal Surveillance Programme is funded by the Federal
health department to provide laboratory analysis of meninococcal isolates in
Australia. The Programme produces an annual report on its findings. This page
contains the annual reports, in both HTML and PDF format, from 1995. These
annual reports are available in PDF format and, for accessibility, in either
HTML or Microsoft Word format.

### [Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD) surveillance in Australia [annual
reports]](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-cjdanrep.htm)

24 February 2023

These annual reports prepared by the Australian National CJD Registry provides
updated Australian human transmissible spongiform encephalopathies (TSE)
figures and discuss a recently published investigation of geographical TSE
clustering in regional New South Wales. These annual reports are available in
PDF format and, for accessibility, in either HTML or Microsoft Word format.

### [Australian Paediatric Surveillance Unit (APSU) annual
reports](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-apsu-annreps.htm)

22 February 2023

The Australian Paediatric Surveillance Unit (APSU) conducts national active
surveillance of rare diseases of childhood, including infectious and vaccine
preventable diseases, genetic disorders, childhood injuries and mental health
conditions. This page contains links to annual reports on summary of
surveillance results for communicable and/or vaccine preventable diseases.
These annual reports are available in PDF format and, for accessibility, in
either HTML or Microsoft Word format.

### [Invasive pneumococcal disease (IPD) in Australia [annual
reports]](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-pubs-annlrpt-
ipdannrep.htm)

09 August 2022

An annual report on invasive pneumococcal disease is produced each year. The
report includes information on incidence of disease and on laboratory
surveillance. This page contains links to the annual reports from 2001.

### [National Arbovirus and Malaria Advisory Committee (NAMAC) annual
reports](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-arboanrep.htm)

09 August 2022

This page contains links to the NAMAC annual reports, which are published in
_Communicable Diseases Intelligence_. The reports also inlcude data on the
Sentinel Chicken Surveillance Programme, which is used to provide an early
warning of increased flavivirus activity in Australia. These annual reports
are available in PDF format and, for accessibility, in either HTML or
Microsoft Word format.

### [National Influenza Surveillance Scheme (NISS) annual
reports](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-pubs-annlrpt-fluannrep.htm)

09 August 2022

The National Influenza Surveillance Scheme includes data from sentinel general
practice consultations for influenza-like illness, laboratory reports of
influenza and absenteeism rates from a national employer. This page links to
annual reports published since 1994.These annual reports are available in PDF
format and, for accessibility, in either HTML or Microsoft Word format.

### [Tuberculosis notifications in Australia [annual
reports]](/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cda-pubs-annlrpt-tbannrep.htm)

09 August 2022

This page contains links to annual reports on tuberculosis notifications in
Australia and reports on bacteriologically confirmed cases and drug
resistance, produced by the Australian Mycobacterium Laboratory Reference
Network from 1994. These reports are available in PDF format and, for
accessibility, in either HTML or Microsoft Word format.
