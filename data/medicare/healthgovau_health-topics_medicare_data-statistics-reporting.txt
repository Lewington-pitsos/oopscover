# Medicare data, statistics and reporting

You can access a wide range of statistics and reports on various aspects of
Medicare, including bulk billing rates, benefits paid and the cost and use of
prescription medicines.

## **Our Medicare data, statistics and reporting**

We publish quarterly and annual Medicare statistics by state, territory and
the Modified Monash Model (MMM) about:

  * bulk billing rates, including by type of service such as general practitioner (GP) attendances, specialist attendances, diagnostic imaging and pathology
  * services claimed
  * benefits paid
  * patient contributions.

We also publish statistics about the cost and usage of medicines supplied
through the [Pharmaceutical Benefits
Scheme](https://www.pbs.gov.au/info/browse/statistics) (PBS).

## **Other Medicare data, statistics and reporting**

You can create your own statistical reports – using different variables, such
as time periods or location – from [Services
Australia](https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/medicare-statistics?context=1)
for:

  * [MBS items](http://medicarestatistics.humanservices.gov.au/statistics/mbs_item.jsp)
  * [MBS categories](http://medicarestatistics.humanservices.gov.au/statistics/mbs_group.jsp)
  * [PBS items](http://medicarestatistics.humanservices.gov.au/statistics/pbs_item.jsp)
  * [PBS groups](http://medicarestatistics.humanservices.gov.au/statistics/pbs_group.jsp) ([Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical](http://www.whocc.no/atcddd) or patient eligibility status).

The Productivity Commission’s annual [Report on Government
Services](https://www.pc.gov.au/research/ongoing/report-on-government-
services) reports on various Medicare statistics, including:

  * details of MBS-funded primary care and mental health services
  * use of Medicare by First Nations people.

The [Australian Institute of Health and
Welfare](https://www.aihw.gov.au/reports-data) reports on various aspects of
the health of Australians, using Medicare data.
