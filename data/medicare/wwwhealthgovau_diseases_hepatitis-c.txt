## About hepatitis C

Hepatitis C is a virus that can cause serious liver disease in an infected
person. The hepatitis C virus is usually transmitted when blood from an
infected person enters the bloodstream of another person (such as through
sharing needles or certain sexual activities).

Disease type:

Bloodborne

Vaccination available under NIP:

No

Notifiable disease:

Yes

## **Prevention, symptoms, diagnosis and treatment**

There is no vaccine to protect against hepatitis C, but there are some very
effective options for the treatment of hepatitis C infection. Treatment
options are available on the Medicare PBS (Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme).

For information about prevention, symptoms, diagnosis and treatment, see
[healthdirect's hepatitis C
page](https://www.healthdirect.gov.au/hepatitis-c).

## **Surveillance and reporting**

Hepatitis C is a [nationally notifiable disease](/topics/communicable-
diseases/nationally-notifiable-diseases "Nationally notifiable diseases").

We monitor cases through the [National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance
System](/our-work/nndss "National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System
\(NNDSS\)").

For more on hepatitis C in Australia, you can [search Communicable Diseases
Intelligence](https://www1.health.gov.au/internet/main/publishing.nsf/Content/cdi-
search).
